Dublin

Attractions

In Dublin, where the ancient and the modern coexist, a thousand years of history come to life. Experience the mood by strolling around Dublin's well-known 17th century Georgian squares. Dublin comes to life at night because of its numerous theaters, concert halls, and movie theaters. A full-fledged Dublin audience truly raises the roof, as many performers will attest.

Dublin, the primary stage in Ireland, is home to a rich tradition of playwrights such as Oscar Wilde, George Bernard Shaw, and Samuel Beckett. Modern Irish plays are still popular, with theater thriving at venues like The Abbey, The Gaiety, and The Tivoli. Dublin attracts both obscure bands and global megastars, and its twelve music venues, including the new O2 Dublin, The Olympia, and The Ambassador, are known for their packed crowds.

Dublin Bus Tours offer a "hop-on, hop-off" system, allowing passengers to explore the city center and nearby attractions. The South Coast & Gardens Tour takes visitors to Powerscourt, a stately mansion in the Wicklow Mountains, while the North Coast & Castle Tour takes them to Malahide Castle and the fishing port of Howth.

Dublin Castle, a historic complex dating back to 934, is a symbol of Ireland's past, serving as a symbol of the Vikings and English, offering a unique insight into Irish history and a significant part of the city's cultural heritage.
