Dublin

Economy

Dublin is the country's economic hub. Dublin serves as the nation's media center in addition to housing the national parliament and the majority of the civil service. Dublin Port is in charge of a significant amount of Ireland's import and export trade, and the city is the hub of most of the country's transportation system.

Numerous global companies, particularly those in “hi-tech” industries including digital media, information technology, financial services, and pharmaceuticals, are based in Dublin. A number of significant Irish public firms, such as Ryanair, DCC plc, AIB Group, and Bank of Ireland have their headquarters in Dublin. Dublin is home to a large number of state-owned and public sector employers in Ireland, including Ireland's national public service broadcaster, several teaching hospitals, utility companies like ESB Group, educational institutions like Trinity College Dublin and Technological University Dublin, and the majority of Ireland's higher courts.

Since 1759, the St. James's Gate Brewery has been producing Guinness, one of Ireland's most well-known alcoholic beverages. Dublin benefited from the meat industry's participation as well. The Dublin region is home to a number of Irelan's most popular tourist destinations, such as the National Gallery of Ireland, the Guinness Storehouse, and the Dublin Zoo. Large-scale data center activities, including international facilities operated by Google, Microsoft, and Amazon, are concentrated in the region around Ireland's capital city.
